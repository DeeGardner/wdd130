The Cookie (Food)

The cookie or biscuit as it is sometimes referred, is a small often thin sweetened cake commonly made with flour, sugar, egg, and butter. Cookies can range from crisp to soft and anywhere in between, they can also range vastly in sweetness, ingredients, size, and decoration.

It is believed by historians that cookies were originally test cakes or small bits of dough that were used to test the temperature of an oven. Cookies have been traced back to as early as the 7th Century A.D. in Persia. As time progressed wars and exploration would spread the cookie to other countries and continents.

Today there are numberless varieties and variations of cookies that have developed, and the name varies in different countries and cultures of the world. Some names include biscuits, biscotti, and galleta.
